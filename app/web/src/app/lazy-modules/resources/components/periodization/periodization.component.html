What Is Nutrition Periodization?
Nutritional Periodization is the planned, purposeful, and strategic use of specific nutrition changes to enhance the adaptations targeted by individual exercise sessions or periodic training plans or to obtain other effects that will enhance performance or appearance in the long term.
You can use periods of weight cycling, known as bulking cutting and maintaining, to improve performance and physical appearance. Macronutrient manipulation occurs during these periods, mainly with changes in fat and carbohydrate intake to induce caloric surpluses or deficits.
There is little evidence to support an ideal nutrition periodization over the long term. We recommend committing to cutting for at least 6-8 weeks to see fat loss and bulking for at least 12-16 weeks to see muscle gain. Both of these goals should be supported by resistance training and cardiovascular training.
