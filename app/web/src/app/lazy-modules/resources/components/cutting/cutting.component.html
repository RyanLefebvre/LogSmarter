Dieting/Cutting Reccomendations
Cutting is a term used to describe a period of time where an individual is purposely eating fewer calories than they need to maintain their weight. This is typically done with the end goal of losing body fat or improving sports performance.
Faster rates of weight loss may risk a greater loss in lean mass but help an individual reduce overall body fat quicker. Slower rates of weight loss may preserve more lean mass but will extend the time period an individual spends in a caloric deficit. Choosing a faster or slower rate of weight loss depends upon many factors including personal preferences, goals, an individual's diet adherence, and inter-individual responses to a hypocaloric diet.
LogSmarter™ offers a suggested cutting range for a daily caloric intake instead of a single value. Within this range, adjustments can be made to meet an individual’s needs if they are losing weight too fast or too slow. LogSmarter™ users can choose between a conservative, moderate, aggressive, or very aggressive approach to cutting. The descriptions below describe the guidelines for which approach to choose if a user is unsure.
The upper and lower bound of the recommended cutting ranges are based upon typical weight loss recommendations made in the literature (Garthe et al, 2011), (Roberts et al, 2020), (Murphy et al, 2015). To construct a goal calorie intake range for fat loss from these recommendations, subtract the upper and lower bounds of the ranges from your TDEE.
Conservative
[200 - 300] kcal
Choose this option if you want to diet slowly, are lean, and want to lose minimal muscle mass.
Moderate
[300 - 500] kcal
Choose this option if you want to diet slowly, are somewhat lean, and fine if you lose a small amount of muscle mass. *We recommend this option if you are unsure*
Aggressive
[500 - 750] kcal
Choose this option if you want to diet quickly, and are not lean. This option would be used for a short-term diet.
Very Aggressive
[750 - 1000] kcal
Choose this option if you want to diet extremely quickly, are overweight or obese, and are prepared to be hungry. This option should only be used for short time periods (4-6 weeks).
